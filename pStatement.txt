I believe respecting a chronological order would be the best thing to do in this case.
I did my pre-university studies in Madrid, in the second generation
under the excellence program of my local institute. After finishing the
scientific-technological bachelorship, I abandoned my aptitude for
study medicine back then and decided to study a double degree with the idea of
becoming an engineer.

Being the Polytechnic University of Madrid the best public university
in relation to engineering degrees, I opted for the path of software
due to my interest in programming.
Although I was offered different specializations during my years of
degree, from the first year it was clear to me that I would specialize in
mathematics applied to computing and so I did, choosing a
minor in complex analysis.
To support this decision, I joined the international mobility program
Magalhaes and I studied advanced math courses at one of the most remarkable
universities in Latin America: The Technological Institute of Monterrey.

It is true that I could not obtain an economic benefit because I only had a 
student visa, nevertheless, I accepted the proposal of my teachers to give 
integration courses and give support to my classmates.
Thanks to my commitment I had the  opportunity to contribute to an active research
project as part of the support team of a neural network in deployment. Although 
I did not participate in the design of the network, my team contributed to
optimize its training.

While I was going through a stage of personal growth, I took the opportunity to study 
some minor certifications on my own, such as: photography, cinematography, psychology 
and music. In retrospect, I can now see that these courses did not leave me all the 
time necessary to achieve excellence in all the subjects at the university, but I don’t
regret it. I do not remember a time when I enjoyed learning so much, complementing my 
mandatory itinerary with new horizons.

I finished my two university degrees by presenting my two final projects,
one dedicated to pure mathematics and the other to software development. Getting

3-> Personal Essay:

1. Rationalism:

Cuando era pequeño, me costaba mucho hablar. Aprendí a leer más rápido que la mayoría, pero por 
alguna razón no era capaz de pronunciar palabra, sobretodo con desconocidos. Mis padres entendieron
algun tiempo después que en realidad estaba aprendiendo, pero no me atrevería a hablar hasta que 
pudiese hacerlo perfectamente. Tal era mi actitud hacia el fracaso y la frustración. Una vez consideré
que mis aptitudes estaban a la altura, exhibía tartamudez y falta de fluidez. Mi mente trabajaba 
demasiado deprisa y la falta de práctica me condenaba a no poder decir todo lo que quería.
Algunos años mas tarde, ya en la escuela, mientras que mis compañeros jugaban a los tazos unos con otros
yo estaba casi siempre solo, leyendo a Nietszche o libros de historia. A menudo frecuentaba el psicólogo
atormentado por miedos irracionales propios de un adolescente mientras que seguía trabajando con mi logopeda.

Mis tutores en aquel momento recomendaron medidas especiales apropiadas para un niño superdotado, pero fue
cuando conocí a Salvador. Mi profesor por los siguientes dos años, y mi mentor por lo siguientes diez. 
Rechazó cualquier trato de favor, y me obligó a integrarme con mis compañeros, enseñandome que todo 
ocurre por una razón y transmitiendome el método cientifico. A partir de entonces desarrollé la necesidad 
de explicar lo que me rodeaba, resolviendo misterios y exhibiendo un interés excesivo por el autodidactismo
y la erudición.

2. Nihilism:

El conocimiento me condenó en mi etapa de rebeldía adolescente, pues las secuelas de mi aislamiento social
se combinaron con una baja inhibición latente. Condición que me hacía propenso a ser más receptivo a los 
estímulos de mi entorno, procesando demasiada información visual, gestual y encontrando patrones naturales.
Me costaba salir de mi entorno de confianza, a más gente a mi alrededor más probabilidad de agobiarme y a más
conocimiento que recabava, más datos estaban a mis disposición agravando mi problema.
Mi solución fue otro arma de doble filo, restarle valor e importancia a las personas y las situaciones. Si nada
importaba demasiado, no había necesidad de culparse y el fracaso despareció por completo. Fue mi época más 
depresiva y oscura, pues no era capaz de rechazar el extremismo y buscar una opción menos radical. Para entonces
mi mentor ya estaba muy mayor y había desarrollado problemas de salud, pero no todo fue necesariamente malo. 
Durante este tiempo, me descubrí a mi mismo y aprendí no solo a aceptarme sino a sentirmo orgulloso de mi mismo,
estudiaba cualquier cosa por mi cuenta, certificandome títulos menores en fotografía, cine, psicología y
música. Si bien estos cursos no me dejaban todo el tiempo necesario para lograr la excelencia en todas mis
asignaturas en la universidad, no recuerdo época donde disfrutase tanto de aprender complementando mi itinerario
obligatorio con nuevos horizontes.

Componiendo música pude ir poco a poco devolviendo importancia a ciertas cosas, comenzando por las personas 
imprescindibles en mi vida, mis principios, siguiendo por mis sueños y terminando en pequeños detalles superficiales
que me hacian feliz.


3. Vitalism:

Hace relativamente poco, me declaré sano emocional y mentalmente, habiendo superado mis problemas para relacionarme
y mis ciclos depresivos por problemas existenciales. Aceptando que la perfección es inalcanzable, es imposible
pero a la vez esta justo delante de nosotros constantemente. Ahora sentía que no solo se trataba de sobrevivir sino 
de aprovechar al máximo mis posibilidades: la vida como criterio de valor absoluto y valor universal.

Hace unas semanas de este momento, mientras escribo de antemano este personal statement, fui al hospital para visitar
a Salvador. No puedo explicar como es posible que en principios de demencia pueda ser capaz de desafiarame al ajedrez, 
como solía hacer cuando era un niño. Le agradecí por haberme salvado y escuché su consejo por última vez. Me acariciaba
mientras me felicitaba por haber concluido mis estudios y haberme convertido en ingeniero, pero me tiró de la oreja 
mientras me dijo que solo eso no era suficiente. No solo era ingeniero, sino que debía atribuirme el rol de impulsor
del cambio, empleando mis habilidades y mi talento para ayudar a otras personas. "Abre un mapa y dime donde está la
frontera de la tecnología, porque allí quiero que vayas".

Al principio me sobrecogió la idea, a quien no le asustaría algo asi. Me desafió a un match de tres partidas, de las 
cuales dos quedaron en tablas, y contra todo pronóstico, perdí la última partida jugando con blancas siendo incapaz de 
responder a su siciliana, reforzada con la experiencia de toda una vida. Declarandome vencido, me transmitió cual era 
su deseo y me encargó realizarlo. Recuerdo salir del hospital con el esbozo de lo que sería mi plan para estudiar en 
uno de los paises más punteros en tecnología, mi viaje y desafío, abrazando una nueva cultura, idioma y posibilidades en 
Corea del Sur.

Rationalism:

When I was little, I had a hard time talking. I learned to read faster than most, but for
for some reason I was not able to utter a word, especially with strangers. My parents understood
some time after that I was discreetly learning, but I wouldn't dare speak until I
I could do it perfectly. Such was my attitude towards failure and frustration. Once I considered
that my skills were up to par, I exhibited stuttering and lack of fluency. My mind worked
too fast and lack of practice condemned me to not being able to say everything I wanted.
A few years later, already at school, while my classmates played pogs with each other
I was almost always alone, reading Nietszche or history books. I often went to the psychologist
tormented by irrational adolescent fears while continuing to work with my speech therapist.

My guardians at the time recommended special measures appropriate for a gifted child, but then
I met Mr. S. My teacher for the next two years, and my mentor for the next ten.He refused any favorable 
treatment, and forced me to integrate with my classmates, teaching me that everything happens for a reason 
and transmitting the scientific method to me. From that point forward I developed the need to explain my 
urroundings, solving mysteries and exhibiting an excessive interest in self-taught and erudition.

Nihilism:

Knowledge condemned me in my stage of adolescent rebellion, because the sequels of my social isolation
were combined with low latent inhibition. Condition that made me prone to be more receptive to
stimuli from my environment, processing too much visual and gestural information and finding natural patterns.
It was difficult for me to leave my trusted boundaries, the more people around me the more likely I would be 
overwhelmed and the more knowledge I acquired, more data was accessible to me, which aggravated the problem.

My solution was another double-edged sword: downplaying people and situations. If nothing
mattered too much, there was no need to blame myself, and the failure was completely erradicated. It was my most
depressive and dark stage by far, because I was not capable of rejecting extremism and looking for a less radical option. 
Despite that even my mentor was already very old and had developed health problems, it was not a hopeless scenario.

During this time, I learned not only to accept myself but also to feel proud of myself. I rescued self-taught as 
modus operandi, being able to gradually restore importance to certain things relying on music composition.

Vitalism:

Relatively recently, I proudly have overcome my socializing problems and my depressive cycles due to existential issues.
By accepting that perfection is unattainable, it is impossible but at the same time it is right in front of us constantly. 
I realized that it was not only a matter of surviving but to make the most of my possibilities: life as a criterion of absolute
and universal value.

A few weeks ago from this moment, I went to the hospital to visit Mr. S. I can't explain how it is possible that in 
early dementia he would be able to challenge me to chess, like I used to do when I was a kid. I thanked him for saving 
me and listened to his advice for the last time.I should not be merely an engineer, but I had to self-attribute the role 
of promoter of change, using my skills and my talent to help other people. "Open a map and tell me where the frontier of 
technology is, because there is where I want you to go".

He challenged me to a match of three games, two of them ended in draws, and against all odds, I lost the last game playing 
with whites being unable to respond to his Sicilian, reinforced with the experience of a lifetime. Declaring myself defeated, 
he transmitted to me what his dream was and commissioned me to realize it. I remember leaving with the outline 
of what would be my plan to study in one of the most advanced countries in technology, my journey and challenge, embracing a 
new culture, language and possibilities in South Korea.
